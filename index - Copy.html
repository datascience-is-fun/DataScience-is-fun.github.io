About Me

Recently, I have been working in projects that aimed at improving the efficiency and quality of healthcare delivery, which include developing machine learning based predictive models to prevent hospital readmissions and improving staff productivity. In my current position as graduate research graduate in New England VERC, I am developing staffing models for anticoagulation clinics in six states of the New England with ultimate goal of spreading nationally. In my dissertation, I am developing hospital inpatient admission control and patient transfer policies in a multi-hospital system that can support decision makers in making inpatient admission and patient transfer decisions.

I have been using several tools for my projects including R, Python, SQL, Matlab, SAS, VBA, and Simio. To sharpen my data science skills, I am currently taking online data science courses from Microsoft, data science specialization from Johns Hopkins University, and big data XSeries from Berkeley. I am currently looking for a position as a data scientist.
